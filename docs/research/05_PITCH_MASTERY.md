## Judge Psychology (4-Min Format)

- First impression: ~100ms (face evaluation), 30 seconds decides 50% of outcome
- **University judges evaluate**: clarity, feasibility, go-to-market, differentiation, risk/ethics, coachability, "wow factor"
- **Not VC pitch**: form is part of the product — judges compare 8-12 finalists back-to-back
- Serial position effect: judges remember BEGINNING and END, forget MIDDLE

## Persuasion Psychology for 8 Slides

### Processing Fluency
- Statements easier to process feel MORE TRUE (Reber & Schwarz 1999)
- Repetition increases fluency → illusion of truth
- **Practical**: one shock fact + one conclusion + pause. NOT three shock facts in a row

### Peak-End Rule
- People retrospectively judge experience by PEAK MOMENT and ENDING
- **Peak** = demo (show trust score appearing) → 3-second pause
- **End** = closed loop back to hook + clear ask

### Loss Aversion (Prospect Theory)
- Losses psychologically "weigh" more than equivalent gains
- **Do**: first loss-frame for urgency ("companies lose..."), then gain-frame for justification ("with us they get...")
- **Don't**: only "GAIN $X" at early-stage — sounds like marketing without defense

### Anchoring (Tversky & Kahneman 1974)
- First number mentioned shifts all subsequent evaluations
- **First anchor**: reliable, verifiable: 233 AI incidents in 2024 (+56%)
- **Second anchor**: McKinsey $2.6-4.4T genAI value; agent economy is layer within

### Social Proof with Zero Customers
- **Design partners**: 3-5 LOI or "pilot interest" letters
- **Public problem validation**: Stanford AI Index + Gartner citations
- **Technical trust-proof**: align with NIST AI RMF, OWASP Top 10
- **Ecosystem context**: "governed agents" = mainstream (OpenAI guides)
- **DO NOT**: fake logos or advisors — instant credibility death

## Delivery for Non-Native Speaker

### Accent Strategy
- Foreign accent reduces perceived credibility via processing difficulty
- **Do**: "I'm an international founder, you'll hear an accent. So I made this pitch maximally simple — and I'll show you a working product."
- **Don't**: "Sorry for my English" — self-sabotage anchoring

### Target Speech Rate
- Normal: 150-190 wpm. Pitch with accent: **130-145 wpm**
- Slides 1-3 (hook/problem/solution): 130-145 wpm for trust
- Demo: slightly faster, but with pauses at "truth moments"
- Finale: slow again

### Pause Map
| Location | Duration | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| After first shock fact (slide 1) | 2 sec | Let number "hit" |
| Before "Carfax for AI" (slide 3) | 1 sec | Prepare for label |
| Before demo start | 1 sec + judge eye contact | Signal control |
| When trust score appears in demo | **3 sec** | **PEAK MOMENT** |
| After ask at end | 2 sec, then "thank you" | Don't let ask drown |

### Filler Words
- Replace "um" with pause + inhale (pause = control, not failure)
- Close mouth during pause (physical stop-trigger), hold eye contact

### Russian Pronunciation Fixes (Highest ROI in 32 Days)
- **Priority**: stress + vowel reduction (affects intelligibility faster than perfecting "th")
- Practice: 10 min/day shadowing YOUR OWN PITCH SCRIPT
- 3 dangerous words: "trust", "score", "agent" — say 50x slow → 50x at target speed

## Body Language

### Stance
- Feet shoulder-width, weight 55/45, soft knees
- Shoulders down-back, chin neutral
- Hands: "neutral frame" at waist/belly, fingers relaxed

### Gesture Map
| Slide | Gesture | Note |
|-------|---------|------|
| 1 (statistics) | "Frame" (palms slightly inward) | Collecting attention |
| 3 (solution) | "Offering" (palms up briefly) | "Here's what we give" |
| 4 (demo) | Minimal — mouse control only | Gesture only at trust score moment |
| 5 (market) | "Scale" (hands slightly wider) | Once, don't wave |
| 8 (ask) | "Point" (palm toward amount/CTA) | Then pause |

### Eye Contact
- 3-5 seconds per judge at key moments
- Scan 4-6 people but HOLD gaze at: after fact, before demo, after trust score, before ask
- During demo click: look at screen only when needed, return to judges for conclusion phrase

### Pre-Stage Ritual (90 seconds before)
- 5 slow breathing cycles (coherent breathing)
- Open posture (not "superhero", just expansive) + even exhale

## Slide Design Rules

- **8-15 words max** per slide (except numbers/labels)
- **One idea + one headline-conclusion** per slide
- **Color**: white/light grey base + dark blue (trust) + red/orange only for negative numbers
- **Font**: 30pt minimum, one sans-serif
- **Animation**: 1 type only across entire deck (e.g., appear on click)
- **Data viz**: 1 metric per chart, direct labels, minimal legend, NO pie charts, NO 3D

## Demo Strategy (45-60 seconds)

### Hybrid Approach (Best for Competition)
- Click 1-2 actions LIVE (marketplace + trust score)
- Keep recorded 30-second backup embedded in slides (one click to activate)

### Demo Protection Kit
- Local copy of demo video on laptop
- Phone hotspot as backup internet
- Offline screenshots of "trust score" screen as last resort

### Wi-Fi Death Script
> "To not waste your time on Wi-Fi, I have a short recording of this step — the point is the same: here's how the trust score changes."

### Making Demo Memorable
1. Show a "character" (agent/seller/buyer)
2. Set stakes ("if the agent lies/harms — who pays?")
3. One action
4. Show trust score + explain "what makes it up"

## Q&A Mastery (2 Minutes)

### Answer Formula: Acknowledge → Answer → Bridge
1. Acknowledge (no defense): "Yes, that's a real risk."
2. Direct answer: 1-2 sentences
3. Bridge to key message: "That's exactly why we..."
- **Don't**: long intros "That's a great question..." — kills time
- **Better**: "Shortly: ..." (and straight to answer)

### "I Don't Know" Script
> "I don't want to guess. I don't have the exact number right now. What I can say confidently: ... [1 fact]. After the pitch I'll send you the exact answer and source."

### "What If Google Builds This?"
- "If Google builds it — that validates the market."
- "We build a neutral trust-layer and data/score portability: value grows from cross-platform nature, not one ecosystem."
- "Our short-term focus — narrow beachhead (segment X), where we collect data and integrations faster."
- **DON'T say**: "Google can't" — looks naive
